The puzzle game sudoku

Written by: Matthew

Originally posted: 23rd of June, 2024
Last updated: 2nd of July, 2025

Sudoku? Isn't that for old people?

Sudoku has a boring ending. Its just a grid filled with numbers. But people love filling in these empty boxes. Why?

The appeal of puzzle games as compared to other games such as traditional sports or esports is that they are more reliant on your brain than your physical body. This is why older folk gravitate towards puzzle games, as their disadvantage of their weaker body's are less of an issue, levelling the playing field. Equality is hard to create in games, and I see puzzle games, especially sudoku, as a leader in this front.

Between different age groups, their is measurable difference in brain development. Therefore from that alone, you could argue that older people are always going to better at puzzle game. Experience is king. The more experience you have, the more successful you are. This applies to all sports, although none more than puzzle games such as sudoku.

The vast majority of sudoku players are older folk. You are unlikely to see a ten year old today engrossed in a game on the bus. This is because it looks boring and the stigma around old people playing them. Even I fell prey to this in my earlier years, trying sudoku way later on in my life when I was trying to learn something new. I would like to see this stigma reduced somewhat, as it is unjustified. Sudoku consists of problem solving, and people will become addicted to that feeling of solving a difficult problem when they do it for the first time.

In sudoku, you are only versing yourself. I haven't heard of any two player variants, although I will admit that I haven't research deeply in this topic. This is one of the main driving factors as to why I play the game. When you play other games, there is this stress to get a good "score" that you don't have in sudoku.

The closest element of stress that sudoku games have is the stopwatch, recording how long it takes for you to solve each puzzle. I find it easy to ignore, thus I have never felt the pressure of time get to me. If it really does bother you though, you can just disable it in settings for most sudoku applications.

People love sudoku because it is arguable more fair for all ages as compared to other types of sports. Therefore, everyone has a equal chance to compete and do well at, creating a welcoming environment. This is the magic of sudoku, and why I believe you should try to complete a grid today if you have never before.
