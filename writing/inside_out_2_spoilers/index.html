Inside Out 2 review (spoilers)

Written by: Matthew

Originally posted: 7th of July, 2024
Last updated: 2nd of July, 2025

Past

Back in 2016, I distinctly remember the feelings I felt when I first watched the original Inside Out.

Apart from Ratatouille (2007), no other movie made me feel so emotional toward its characters and plot. I really enjoyed it, and I considered it as one of my all time favourite movies.

When I first discovered that a sequel was in the works while I was reading the Wikipedia article, I was pretty excited about it.

My first impressions on the trailer when it first released were okay. I already recognised that the sequel would never be better than the original, but there was nothing in there that put me off. I presume that is the best outcome from the audience that you could get from a first impressions trailer.

After a few more teasers came out, I looked at some comments. A few noted that a scene in the movie looked awfully similar to a past online incident involving Rainbow Dash and a jar. I didn't understand it so naturally, I looked it up. Thank internet, that part will now be spoiled forever. I can't blame them too much, I wasn't force to search it up or anything. Curiosity killed the cat.

Present

I was confused when it finished. I needed it to be explained to me before I understood the message. I think that it was partly due to the pacing of the story, it was too fast for me. Confusingly, I didn't have any problems following the plot of the original Inside Out so I don't know this is all about.

The stand out line out of the entire movie was at the end of act 2, where Joy says that you might feel less joy as you grow up. It got to me pretty good, and I would even argue that it had more gravity than than even the final climax. I didn't cry at this part, although I could definitely see other people fall into tears for just this one scene.

Animation was top tier as usual, even for Pixar standards. My favourite small detail was the way that the jar refracted the light in the background when the gang was trapped in the jar. You can most clearly see it when the camera is at POV angle where Joy is looking out. The scene where they ride on top of all of the "bad" memories back to headquarters was also really cool. It was liquid simulation, just much less precise.

The call back to the 1984 commercial was an unexpected but welcoming surprise. It felt pretty natural to include in that scene; not tacked on.

Future

I watched Schaffrillas's review on Inside Out 2 right after I watched the movie. I agreed with most things he said, such as envy not getting the screen time she needed to fully flesh out her character.

As of the time of writing, Inside Out 2 has made just over a billion in the box office and rated a 7.9/10 on IMDB. Needless to say, the movie has been a huge success and I wouldn't be surprised if the series were to become a trilogy.
