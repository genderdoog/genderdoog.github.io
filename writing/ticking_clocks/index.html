Ticking clocks

Written by: Matthew

Originally posted: 30th of October, 2023
Last updated: 30th of June, 2025

Time, Mr Matthew?

It was that time of the year again, where your relatives dump a load of clothes that no longer fit them, and onto the front porch. This time however, they also included other stuff. I guess that this year they really wanted to clear everything out.

While digging through this mess and sorting out the boring items from the not so boring items, I found a small blue analogue clock. It was missing its battery door cover, but otherwise seemed to tick fine when I placed a fresh new AA battery inside. I placed it in my bedroom, then went on about the rest of my day like usual.

When I finally switched off the lights late at night, I could hear it. TICK, TICK, TICK. You don't realise how annoying that small of a sound can be to your ability to fall to sleep when it is the only noise making thing in the room. But man, trying to sleep that night was a pretty awful experience.

As the days went by, the sound gradually slowly tuned out of my brain each night, and I soon regained my ability to sleep again without it being too disruptive. Of course the sound was still being made, and I can focus my ears on it to hear it, but now I have to try to listen to it, and it isn't the other way around.

This whole ordeal made me wonder about something, how much things have you not noticed simply because your body tuned out? Sure, most of the stuff that your body tunes out is usually annoying or monotonous sounds, however there is surely somewhere in your life where it was a detriment, rather than a power.
